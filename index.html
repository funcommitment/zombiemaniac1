Dead Snow is a 2009 Norwegian comedy horror film directed by Tommy Wirkola. This signature blend of gore and humor with this Nazi-zombie epic movie. And you know Nazis have always been the ultimate villains in movies. Combine that with zombies and you really get something that no one would sympathise with. The premise of the film is similar to that of the draugr, a Scandinavian folkloric undead greedily protecting its (often stolen) treasures.With some bloody scenes that will make your night sleepless. 9.
One cut of the Dead



One Cut of the Dead (カメラを止めるな!, Kamera o Tomeru na!, transl. "Don't Stop the Camera!") is a 2017 Japanese[1][2] independent zombie comedy film written and directed by Shin'ichirō Ueda. It grossed US $27,935,711 (¥3.12 billion) in Japan and $30.5 million worldwide, making box office history by earning over a thousand times its budget.[3] The film received acclaim from critics, who praised its originality, writing and humor. This indigenous Japanese horror-comedy tells the tale of an ill-fated film crew who , while shooting a low-budget zombie movie in a decrepit WWII-era ,are set upon by hordes that are actually undead. This is a brilliant zombie movie with comedy that will make you laugh and surprisingly emotional.

28 days later



28 Days Later is a 2002 British post-apocalyptic survival horror film directed by Danny Boyle and written by Alex Garland. It stars Cillian Murphy as a bicycle courier who awakens from a coma to discover the accidental release of a highly contagious, aggression-inducing virus has caused the breakdown of society . He soon joins up with fellow survivors Selena as well as Frank and his daughter Hannah ,with whom he prepares to navigate their dangerous new world. 28 Days Later is credited with reinvigorating the zombie genre of horror film and influencing a revival in the decade after its release, with its fast-running infected and character-driven drama. Zombieland: Double Tap is a 2019 American post-apocalyptic zombie comedy film directed by Ruben Fleischer. Ten years after the events of the first Zombieland movie, our heroes - Tallahassee, Wichita, Columbus and Little Rock - are enjoying life in the White House. Then, all of a sudden, Wichita and Little Rock leave. Wichita eventually returns but Little Rock is charting her own course. Tallahassee, Wichita and Columbus set out to find her, accompanied by Columbus's new girlfriend, of sorts, Madison. In this horror comedy, the crew fights off evolved zombies, meet up with other survivors and must find a missing Little Rock. . It's fun to see that even after ten years, a film like this hasn't lost its charm. Yes, it's a zombie flick but "Zombieland: Double Tap" is packed with laughs, heart and epic kills.


5.
Shaun of dead



Shaun of the Dead is a 2004 romantic zombie comedy film directed by Edgar Wright and written by Wright and Simon Pegg. Pegg stars as Shaun, a downtrodden London salesman who is caught alongside his loved ones in a zombie apocalypse. Shaun is a 30-something loser with a dull, easy existence. When he's not working at the electronics store, he lives with his slovenly best friend, Ed, in a small flat on the outskirts of London. The only unpredictable element in his life is his girlfriend, Liz, who wishes desperately for Shaun to grow up and be a man. When the town is inexplicably overrun with zombies, Shaun must rise to the occasion and protect both Liz and his mother.Shaun of the Dead cleverly balances scares and witty satire, making for a bloody good zombie movie with loads of wit.
